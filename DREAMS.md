## 🤖 AI Co-pilot

### Natural-language chore creation
Turn conversational phrases like "every other Tuesday, take out recycling" into structured tasks automatically. Why it'd matter: speeds up task entry and lowers friction for family members who'd rather speak than tap.

### AI-written weekly recap narrative
Turn dry weekly stats (e.g., "23 chores done, 5-star streak, 3 trades") into a fun, personalized story. Why it'd matter: makes recaps feel celebratory instead of utilitarian; KinTeam already has Claude vision edge functions to build on.

### Fairness coach
Analyze household workload balance over weeks/months and suggest rebalancing (e.g., "Maya's had 30% more tasks than Ethan this month; suggest shift X chores to rebalance"). Why it'd matter: catches bias in task assignment and helps parents stay aware of fairness dynamically.

## 💡 Other parked ideas

### Allowance / pocket-money ledger
Convert stars earned into real money (e.g., 100 stars = $5). Track payouts and balances per member. Why it'd matter: connects gamification to tangible reward and teaches money management.

### Notifications & reminders
Push/email alerts for due chores, pending approvals, streaks about to break, or trades waiting. Why it'd matter: keeps family in sync without requiring constant app checks; no notification layer exists today.

### Photo-proof completion gallery
Surface photos uploaded for AI chore verification as a shared memories feed (timestamped, by member, searchable by chore). Why it'd matter: creates a warm, journalistic record of family effort and life over time.

### Per-member real auth + installable PWA
Replace current shared-device PIN with OAuth/email login per person. Installable PWA so the app lives on home screens. Why it'd matter: enables multi-household support and higher trust for trusted access; currently anonymous session only.

### Flexible scheduling
Support daily, biweekly, monthly, or custom-cadence chores beyond the single Monday weekly reset. Why it'd matter: captures real-world rhythm (trash on Thursdays, weeding monthly, etc.) and reduces forced workarounds.
